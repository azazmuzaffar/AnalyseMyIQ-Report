What is IQ?

At its core, IQ is a measure of your ability to reason. What makes IQ so unique is that it basis itself off of all the previous scores achieved on that particular assessment (with the aim to make 100 the most common score) and variations of an IQ test are used to determine:

  • A A prediction of how well a child will perform academically
  • RThe effectiveness of ADHD treatments
  • QHow effective a potential employee will be

Despite being so widely used, IQ has become a controversial subject with one of the main criticisms being that it isn’t a good predictor of success.

We agree.

It’s not how smart you are, it’s what you do with it that matters.

The problem that we’ve found is that smart people tend to be too smart for their own good. For smart people, life can feel like a child in a sweet shop, with unlimited choices for what they can accomplish with their lives.

Our assessment is designed to cut down on those choices and help you discover how to leverage what you are already good at so that you can do something great.

How is IQ Calculated?

Once you’ve completed the IQ Test your results are compared with data from other participants in your age group who have already taken the test and then a “normalized” score is calculated.

“Normalizing” means your final score is relative to the score most commonly achieved by your age group. For you Verbal archetypes, this means that anything higher than 100 means you got more correct answers than the average.

Now to explain it numerically, if most people answered 20/36 correctly then anyone who answers 26 correctly will have an IQ of 100. Anyone who answers more than 26 correctly will have an IQ higher than 100 and anyone that answers fewer than 26 correctly will have an IQ lower than 100.
